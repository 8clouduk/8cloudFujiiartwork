Sunday Morning

Monologue

Sunday morning in Mannington.
Rain against the window, steady and unhurried.

There’s something spiritual here, something quietly healing.
The most beautiful place isn’t a landmark — it’s the stillness.

Morning light breaks through, sunshine one moment, pouring rain the next.
Both feel honest. Both feel necessary.

I’m not looking for certainty.
I’ve learned better than that.

I just don’t want this moment to disappear
before I’ve had the chance to breathe.

Even the flowers seem to understand it —
the most beautiful one in Mannington
standing without asking to be noticed.

Soon enough, the day will ask things of us.
Responsibilities, noise, the usual distance we keep.

But for now,
if you’re still here, present, unguarded —
that’s more than enough.
